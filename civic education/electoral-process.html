Electoral Process in Kenya

The electoral process in Kenya is a crucial component of the country's democratic system. It ensures that citizens have a voice in choosing their representatives and shaping the direction of the nation. In this section, we'll walk you through the key steps in the Kenyan electoral process, from voter registration to ballot counting.

Voter Registration

The first step in the electoral process is voter registration. Kenyan citizens who are at least 18 years old can register to vote at designated registration centers or online through the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) website. During registration, voters provide personal information, such as their name, date of birth, and national ID number, and have their fingerprints captured for identification purposes.

Ballot Counting and Results

After the polls close, election officials at each polling station begin the ballot counting process. They first reconcile the number of voters who signed in against the number of ballots cast. They then sort the ballots by candidate and tally the votes. The results are then transmitted to the IEBC's national tallying center, where the final results are announced.
